Final Checklist

Use this checklist right before submitting any prompt to reduce QA rejections. Every item should be “yes.”

  • The prompt uses at least four real reasoning layers, each contributing to narrowing down the answer, with supporting web sources.
  • The question is complex but solvable by an expert with web search; it cannot be answered by a simple, direct Google query alone.
  • The answer is string‑matchable, evergreen, unambiguous, and not easily guessable.
  • No AI tools, plagiarism, counting tricks, non‑text sources, or step‑by‑step instructions are used.
  • Gemini Pro 2.5 with Google grounding fails three out of three times, and you have at least one screenshot showing the failure and grounding on.
  • Rubrics explain each reasoning step and include correct English‑language citations that can be accessed without extra clicks (no navigation‑heavy pages).
  • The high‑level plan is a clean sequence of “Verify / Find / Cross‑reference” steps that, if followed, lead from the question to the final answer.
  • The wording is professional, grammatical, and polished enough for a production‑quality dataset.
